The environment of birch and its adaptations

This is a very important part of plants that i nearly forgot!

Birch is a tree that normally grows around lakes and streams. It grows in the tundras
and surprisingly easily grows in areas destroyed by wildfires. Birch trees will mostly grow in Europe
and even more in Northern Europe.

How has birch adapted to it environment in the tundra

Birch has adpated to the tundra over time. Over time birches branches have
become more flexible the allow snow to fall on them and not break the branches.
Birch also has small sized leaves to make sure there is minimum water loss, This
helps birch maintain its photosynthetic ability. Birch has thick bark to protect
the inner parts of itself and to stop decaying. Birch also widly spreads out the leaves
to obtain more oxygen. These are most of the adapataions that birch has gone through.
